Frequently Asked Questions

How does the temperature alert system work?

Our app monitors real-time temperature data and compares it to historical averages. When temperatures are 10°F or more above normal, you'll receive an alert so you can wear your "IT'S TOO HOT!" shirt. This creates a coordinated visual protest where everyone wears the shirt on the same days - the days when it's ten degrees above average.

Why aren't I receiving notifications?

Make sure you've granted notification permissions to the app in your device settings. Also check that you've enabled location services, as we need your location to provide accurate temperature alerts.

Where does the temperature data come from?

We use data from the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) and compare current temperatures to historical averages for your location. When temperatures are 10°F or more above normal, you'll receive an alert so you can wear your "IT'S TOO HOT!" shirt and join the coordinated visual protest.

How can I help raise climate awareness?

When you receive a temperature alert, wear your "IT'S TOO HOT!" shirt and share the alert on social media. This creates a coordinated visual protest where everyone wears the shirt on the same days - the days when it's ten degrees above average. This collective action helps raise awareness about climate change.

Is my data private and secure?

Yes, we take privacy seriously. We only collect the minimum data needed to provide temperature alerts. Your location data is used solely for weather monitoring and is not shared with third parties.
